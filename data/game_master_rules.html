The Game Master is an optional fifth seat at the table. It's not a player in the rules sense — it's a pseudo-player that controls permanents transferred to it by the Deck of Many Things, attacks when those permanents tell it to, and otherwise sits in silence at the head of the table while the actual players race each other.

GM Mode turns DoMT from "spice the players add to their decks" into "the Deck itself takes a seat." It's the difference between rolling on a chaos table and playing against the dungeon.

When to use it

GM Mode is opt-in. Decide before the game starts whether the table is playing with GM Mode on.

  • GM Mode ON (recommended for 3+ players, 4-player Commander): The Game Master card enters the shared command zone at game start. All cards that transfer permanents to "a random opponent" or "another player" send those permanents to the GM instead.
  • GM Mode OFF (1v1 or casual): The Game Master card is left in the box. Cards behave as written.

The mode affects roughly 30 cards across the four sub-decks. Most cards play the same either way; differences cluster around control-transfer effects.

Setup

  1. Place the Game Master card face-up in the shared command zone, visible to all players.
  2. The GM has no library, hand, graveyard, or exile zone of its own.
  3. Designate a physical area on the table as the GM Zone where its controlled permanents go.
  4. The GM has no life total and does not draw an opening hand.
  5. The GM does not draw cards from the Deck of Many Things on its own — only player-triggered effects send things to it.

Attack target selection

  1. If the card that gave the creature to the GM specifies a target (e.g., "That creature must attack you each combat"), the creature attacks that specified player.
  2. If no target is specified, the GM creature attacks a player chosen uniformly at random from the remaining players (excluding the GM).
  3. If a creature has menace, trample, or other combat keywords, those apply normally during the defending player's blocking step.
  4. If multiple GM creatures attack with no specified target, roll independently for each — they may attack different players.

Combat edge cases

  • Multiple GM creatures attacking the same player. Allowed. Independently rolled or specified target.
  • GM creature with vigilance. Doesn't tap when attacking; can be re-attacked next combat.
  • GM creature dies. Goes to its owner's graveyard, not the GM's zone — the GM has no graveyard. Owner = the player who originally cast or generated it.
  • GM creature exiled. Goes to its owner's exile zone, same logic.
  • GM gains a triggered ability that requires a choice (e.g., "draw a card or gain 3 life"). The GM cannot make the choice. The trigger does not happen — it fizzles. (House rule alternative: choice goes to the GM-affecting card's original controller. Pick one and stick with it.)
  • GM permanent with an activated ability requiring a choice. GM cannot activate it. Mana abilities still produce mana, which burns at end of GM turn.

Win/Loss conditions

  • The GM cannot win or lose. It is neutral pressure on the table.
  • Players win and lose against each other normally. Last player standing wins.
  • If a card or effect would cause the GM to "lose the game" or "win the game," it is ignored.
  • If the last two players are eliminated simultaneously and the GM controls living permanents, the game is a draw. The GM does not "win by being last."

House rule recommendations

  • GM as Aggressor (default): All GM creatures attack on every combat if able.
  • GM as Spectator: GM creatures only attack when their card-of-record specifically says they must. Less hostile; good for casual tables.
  • GM as Auctioneer: When a GM creature would attack and no target is specified, the active player chooses the target. Faster; more political.

Pick one and stick with it for the game.

FAQ — quick reference

Does the GM count for "each player" effects?
No. The GM is a pseudo-player; "each player" / "all players" / "opponents" all exclude the GM unless explicitly stated.
Can I cast counterspells or removal at the GM's permanents?
Yes. The GM's permanents are valid targets even though the GM is not.
Can the GM be milled?
No. The GM has no library.
What happens if a card says "each opponent loses 3 life"?
Real opponents lose 3; GM is unaffected.
"Create a token under target opponent's control" — can I target the GM?
No. "Target opponent" requires a real player.
The Tangled Briar makes plant tokens "under a random opponent's control" — is that the GM in GM Mode?
Yes. All "random opponent gains control" effects route to the GM. The plants still attack the Briar's controller per the card's existing rule.
What if a card has no GM-mode entry in the table above?
Play it as written. Most cards are unaffected by GM Mode.
Can the GM card itself be targeted or destroyed?
No. The GM card sits in the shared command zone and is not a permanent — it's a rules card. Removing it ends GM Mode for the rest of the game.

House-rule adjustments are encouraged — DoMT is a sandbox, not a tournament format.
