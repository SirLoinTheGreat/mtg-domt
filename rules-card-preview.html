Deck of Many Things Rules
Commander Variant

What is the Deck of Many Things? A legendary artifact of incredible power, chaos, and fate. Drawing from this deck will forever change your destiny — for better or worse.
DRAW AT YOUR OWN RISK!

Setup —
  • Shuffle the Deck of Many Things and place it in the command zone.
  • Each player starts with 1 Fate Point and can only have 1 Fate Point maximum.
  • Optional Rule: Each player may draw one non-declared card from the Deck of Many Things at the start of the game without spending a Fate Point.
When You Can Draw From The Deck —

Only a seasoned adventurer may draw from the deck. During each player's fifth and subsequent turns, once during your precombat main phase, you may spend a Fate Point and draw a card from the Deck of Many Things.

  • Optional Rule: When drawing from the deck, you may instead declare how many cards you wish to draw (1 to 4 cards) instead of one. Effects happen in the order drawn.
Optional Gameplay:

Gaining Fate Points — When you have no Fate Points, you may choose to roll 1d6 during your main phase.

On a 6: Gain 1 Fate Point

If you would gain a Fate Point but already have one, you may instead draw a card from the Deck of Many Things.

Card Resolution —

When a card is drawn from the Deck of Many Things, it is cast immediately without paying its mana cost. Drawing from the deck cannot be countered, doesn't use the stack, and can only be responded to by a card Named The Fates.

  • When a card drawn from the Deck of Many Things would go anywhere other than the battlefield, unless explicitly stated on the card as part of its mechanics, shuffle it into the Deck of Many Things, except for cards named The Jester or The Fool.
  • If multiple cards are draw, resolve each card completely in sequence before moving on to the next card.
  • Emblems created by the Deck of Many Things cannot be removed except by effects that specifically say they can remove emblems.
  • The Legend rule does not apply to permanents drawn from the Deck of Many Things.

"Sometimes the cards you're dealt become the fate you're given."

Revised
Deck of Many Things Rules
Commander Variant

A legendary artifact of incredible power, chaos, and fate. What you reveal may change your destiny — for better or worse.

Setup
  • Shuffle the Deck of Many Things and place it in the command zone.
  • Each player starts with one fate point. A player can't have more than one fate point.
Drawing from the Deck

Beginning on each player's fifth turn, once during your precombat main phase, you may spend a fate point. If you do, reveal the top card of the Deck of Many Things and cast it without paying its mana cost. (This is a special action. It doesn't use the stack and can't be responded to except by a card named The Fates.)

Card Resolution
  • Spells cast from the Deck of Many Things can't be countered.
  • Whenever a card that was cast from the Deck would be put into a graveyard, hand, library, or exile from the battlefield, shuffle it into the Deck instead. (Exception: Cards named The Jester or The Fool are exiled instead.)
  • If you cast multiple cards from the Deck, resolve each completely before revealing the next.
  • The legend rule doesn't apply to permanents cast from the Deck of Many Things.
  • Emblems created by the Deck of Many Things can be removed only by cards that specifically say they can.
Fate Glimpsed Once

A player can't cast the same card from the Deck of Many Things more than once per game. If you would reveal a card you've already cast from the Deck this game, shuffle it back and reveal another.

The Deck Vanishes

Once every player has cast at least one card from the Deck of Many Things, exile the Deck. No further cards can be drawn from it this game.

Optional Rules

Free First Draw — During setup, each player may reveal the top card of the Deck and cast it without spending a fate point.

Multi-Draw — When you spend a fate point, you may declare a number from 1 to 4. Reveal and cast that many cards instead of one. You can't stop early, even if a card's effect would remove you from the game.

Gambling for Fate — During your main phase, if you have no fate points, you may roll a d6. On a 6, you gain a fate point. If you would gain a fate point but already have one, you may reveal the top card of the Deck and cast it instead.

"Sometimes the cards you're dealt become the fate you're given."

What Changed

1. "Draw" → "Reveal and cast"
"draw a card from the Deck of Many Things"
"reveal the top card of the Deck of Many Things and cast it"
Avoids conflict with MTG's specific meaning of "draw" (put into hand from library).
2. Special action (Fate Point spend)
"you may spend a Fate Point and draw a card"
"(This is a special action. It doesn't use the stack and can't be responded to except by a card named The Fates.)"
Properly defines timing using MTG's special action framework (like Morph).
3. "Cast immediately" → proper template
"it is cast immediately without paying its mana cost"
"cast it without paying its mana cost"
"Immediately" isn't MTG terminology. The special action framing handles the timing.
4. Replacement effect — proper zones
"would go anywhere other than the battlefield"
"would be put into a graveyard, hand, library, or exile from the battlefield"
Names specific zones per MTG convention.
5. Optional rules separated
All three optional rules moved below a visible divider with keyword names: Free First Draw, Multi-Draw, Gambling for Fate. No longer interleaved with core rules.
6. Typo fixed
"If multiple cards are draw"
"If you cast multiple cards from the Deck, resolve each completely"
8. Multi-Draw is now a binding commitment
"you may instead declare how many cards you wish to draw (1 to 4 cards)"
"You can't stop early, even if a card's effect would remove you from the game."
Matches D&D's original Deck, where declaring draws is a binding contract with fate. This is what makes the Deck terrifying.
7. Tighter text overall
Removed "DRAW AT YOUR OWN RISK!", "Only a seasoned adventurer may draw from the deck", and other flavor from the rules body. Kept the flavor quote at the bottom. This freed up space for clearer templating.
9. New: Fate Glimpsed Once
"A player can't cast the same card from the Deck of Many Things more than once per game. If you would reveal a card you've already cast from the Deck this game, shuffle it back and reveal another."
Imports the D&D rule "no card can be received more than once by the same drawer." Each player gets a unique journey through the Deck.
10. New: The Deck Vanishes
"Once every player has cast at least one card from the Deck of Many Things, exile the Deck. No further cards can be drawn from it this game."
Imports the D&D rule "when the last player at the table has drawn, the deck will disappear." Creates table-wide tension: the holdout's draw closes the Deck for everyone, so aggressive drawers race to pull more cards before the table forces it shut. (One-hour real-time clock from D&D dropped — no wall-clock mechanics in MTG.)
